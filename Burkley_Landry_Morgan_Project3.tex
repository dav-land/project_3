\section{Introduction} \label{APPM2360proj01sec01}

Population dynamics depend on a large number of factors, and there are many ways to model the relationship between predator and prey. The populations of predators and prey are interdependent, with too many or too few of one causing spikes or dips in the other. Furthermore, there are external factors that can influence the populations, resulting in more complicated, and more accurate, models.
